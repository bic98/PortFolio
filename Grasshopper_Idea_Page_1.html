02_How to use vector multiplication.

Key Ideas:

1. After performing a join and then using explode, all curves are aligned in a clockwise direction.

2. The dot product of two vectors is positive when the angle is acute and negative when the angle is obtuse.

Key Idea 1 : After performing a "join curves" and then using "explode", all curves are aligned in a clockwise direction.

You can see all the lines aligned in a clockwise direction. but you cannot know where the first "Index" is

Key Idea 2 : The dot product of two vectors is positive when the angle is acute and negative when the angle is obtuse.

Dot Product Formula 1 :

The dot product of two vectors \( \vec{a} \) and \( \vec{b} \) can also be expressed as the sum of the products of their components:

$$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1 \cdot b_1 + a_2 \cdot b_2 + \dots + a_n \cdot b_n $$


  • \( \vec{a} = \begin{pmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ \vdots \\ a_n \end{pmatrix} \) is the vector \( \vec{a} \).
  • \( \vec{b} = \begin{pmatrix} b_1 \\ b_2 \\ \vdots \\ b_n \end{pmatrix} \) is the vector \( \vec{b} \).


  • The dot product is the sum of the products of corresponding components.

    • So you can see that the value calculated by the multiplication component is the same as the value obtained using the dot product component.

    Dot Product Formula 2 :

    The dot product of two vectors \( \vec{a} \) and \( \vec{b} \) is also equal to the product of the magnitudes of the vectors and the cosine of the angle between them:





    $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \cos \theta $$


  • If \( \theta \) is an acute angle, the dot product is positive.

  • If \( \theta \) is an obtuse angle, the dot product is negative.
